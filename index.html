Every retreat asks you to choose.

Most wellness retreats ask mothers to leave their kids at home. Most family trips ask mothers to set their own needs aside entirely. And almost none hold what it actually means to raise a child between two cultures — homesick for a place your child has never even seen, trying to pass down a language that's slipping, missing a village of aunties who would have helped you do this. Aangan is a retreat built to hold all of it at once: you, your child, and the culture you're both still learning to carry.

THE AANGAN MODEL

Three kinds of time, one weekend.

अम्मा · for you

Amma Time

Circles, movement, and rest with other mothers who get it — the isolation, the mental load, the quiet ache of missing home. Space to be a woman again, not just someone's mother.

खेल · for them

Khel Time

A culturally rooted kids' program — storytelling in your mother tongue, folk games, art, and music — led by caregivers who share their heritage, not a generic kids' club.

संग · together

Sang Time

Cooking, dance, and ritual passed hand to hand, plus simple unstructured time to just be a family — away from the noise, close to where you both came from.

WHERE THIS IS HEADED

One home base. Then anywhere.

Aangan starts as a single retreat you travel to. Over time, it becomes a retreat that travels with you — wherever that is.

NOW

Home Base Retreats

One fixed location, run a few times a year, so we can get the Amma / Khel / Sang model right before we take it anywhere else.

NEXT

Travel Retreats — Domestic & International

Aangan plans the trip — a lake town two hours away, or a village back home you haven't visited in years — and arranges vetted local nannies at the destination to run the Khel camp: games, activities, and culturally rooted playtime for your kids. You still get Amma Time, Sang Time, and real rest, no matter where the retreat lands.

LATER

On-Demand, Anywhere

The same trusted, vetted nanny network available for your own family trips — local or international — so you can travel outside of Aangan retreats and still get real childcare support.

A NOTE FROM ME

I'm building Aangan because every "mom and me" retreat I found didn't hold the parts of me shaped by immigration, a language I'm slowly losing, and a childhood I want my kids to at least taste.

I don't have every detail figured out yet — the location, the exact dates, the price. What I do have is the shape of it, and a belief that it should be built with the women who need it, not just for them. If this is you, join the list below and tell me what would make you say yes.
